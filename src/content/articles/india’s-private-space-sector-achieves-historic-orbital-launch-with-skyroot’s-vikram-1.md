**SRIHARIKOTA, Andhra Pradesh** — Skyroot Aerospace became the **first Indian private company** to design, build, and independently operate an orbital launch vehicle after its **Vikram-1** rocket successfully placed multiple satellites into Low Earth Orbit on a mission designated **Aagaman**, launched from the **Satish Dhawan Space Centre**.

The Hyderabad-based firm confirmed that all primary payloads — a mix of commercial and institutional small satellites and technology demonstrators — were deployed as planned, and that post-insertion checks were completed without incident.

## Mission Profile

Vikram-1 followed a standard multi-stage ascent profile. The vehicle's lower stages ignited and separated on schedule, after which the liquid-fuelled upper stage executed a final burn to insert the payload stack into its target orbit. Range safety and telemetry systems monitored the vehicle throughout ascent, and ground teams confirmed clean payload separation shortly after insertion.

## Vehicle Design

Vikram-1's structure relies on lightweight composite materials for its airframe and stage bodies, engineered to withstand ascent loads while minimizing mass. Its propulsion system pairs solid-fuel motors in the early stages with a liquid-propellant upper stage for precise orbital insertion; the upper-stage engine includes additively manufactured components, reducing production time for complex parts.

The vehicle's guidance, navigation, and control suite integrates inertial measurement units, GPS, and real-time telemetry, with redundant avionics pathways built in for fault tolerance. Ahead of the flight, Skyroot conducted subscale testing, propulsion hot-fire trials, full systems integration checks, and non-destructive evaluation of composite structures.

## Payload Objectives

Beyond demonstrating orbital insertion, the mission validated the upper stage's restart capability for precision orbit placement and confirmed the reliability of payload separation mechanisms. Company officials said the successful deployment of customer payloads establishes Vikram-1's viability for commercial small-satellite missions.

## Regulatory and Commercial Context

The launch was conducted in coordination with national range safety authorities and relevant space agencies, meeting requirements for launch licensing, range safety, and orbital debris mitigation.

Industry analysts say the flight opens a new market segment for Indian launch services, particularly for small satellites and satellite constellations, and strengthens India's position as a competitive option for domestic and international customers. The mission is also expected to bolster investor confidence in India's space startup sector and accelerate downstream partnerships in satellite manufacturing and mission operations.

## Next Steps

Skyroot said it will conduct a detailed post-flight review of telemetry data, including structural and thermal performance and validation of separation and deployment events. Findings from the review are expected to inform design refinements and reliability upgrades as the company works toward a regular cadence of commercial launches.

The mission marks a significant step for India's private space sector, which is expected to see increased competition and investment as more domestic launch providers move from demonstration flights to commercial operations.
