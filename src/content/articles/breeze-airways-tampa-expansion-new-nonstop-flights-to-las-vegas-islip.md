Breeze Airways is deepening its footprint at **Tampa International Airport** **(TPA)** with two fresh nonstop routes — one to the country's leisure-travel capital and one to a long-overlooked corner of the New York market. The low-cost carrier has added service between Tampa and both **Harry Reid International Airport (LAS)** in Las Vegas and **Long Island MacArthur Airport (ISP)** in Islip, New York, giving Florida Gulf Coast travelers two more nonstop options and pushing Breeze into direct competition with several established carriers.

## **The New Routes**

The Tampa–Islip route is the more notable of the two additions, since Breeze has never previously served this market. The twice-weekly flights are scheduled to launch on January 6, and tickets are already available for booking. Islip offers travelers a smaller, less congested alternative to New York's major airports while still providing access to the greater Long Island region.

The Tampa–Las Vegas route, meanwhile, will operate daily starting January 5, adding one of the largest leisure destinations in the United States to Breeze's growing Florida network.





## **Why Islip, and Why Now**

According to data cited from aviation analytics firm Enilria, demand on the Tampa–Islip route has surged over the past year. Passenger volume climbed from just 79 passengers daily each way (PDEW) to 274 PDEW for the year ending March 2026 — a jump that appears to have opened the door for another competitor. Notably, that growth came even as average roundtrip fares fell from $382 to $331, suggesting that added capacity and competition have helped stimulate demand rather than suppress it.

Breeze won't have the market to itself, however. Frontier Airlines currently dominates the Tampa–Islip corridor, holding 58% of traffic for the year ending March 2026, followed by Southwest at 27% and JetBlue at 11%. The split in where passengers start their trips is fairly even — 56% originate in Islip versus 44% in Tampa — but Frontier leads on both ends, with an even larger 60% share among Tampa-originating travelers.

Scheduled capacity on the route also fluctuates seasonally: the market is set to average seven weekly flights in August 2026, dropping to five weekly flights by November, with Frontier flying five weekly A320neo services and Southwest offering two weekly Boeing 737 flights in peak season.

For Breeze to succeed, it will need to either pull passengers away from these entrenched competitors or help grow the market further — a strategy the airline has used successfully in other underserved or thinly contested routes.





## **A Different Kind of Opportunity in Las Vegas**

While Islip represents an underserved niche market, Las Vegas is the opposite: a massive, high-demand leisure hub that virtually every major carrier already serves. Breeze's decision to add daily service there reflects a different growth logic — rather than seeking out overlooked routes, the airline is chasing volume and visibility in one of the country's top leisure destinations.

Together, the two additions show how Breeze is pursuing growth on two fronts at once: broad-appeal, high-traffic leisure markets like Las Vegas, and smaller, geographically concentrated markets like Islip where nonstop options remain relatively limited.





## **Tampa's Growing Role in Breeze's Network**

These additions reinforce Tampa's status as an increasingly important hub in Breeze's rapidly expanding network. The airline has also been building out Tampa's international presence, with the airport set to become a gateway to as many as 14 international routes as part of Breeze's broader expansion.

Founded in 2018 and launched into service in 2021 by JetBlue founder **David Neeleman**, Breeze has grown quickly by leaning on its expanding Airbus A220 fleet to open routes that larger legacy carriers often overlook or underserve. The Tampa additions are the latest sign that the "premium low-cost" carrier continues to see room for growth in Florida, even as it wades into markets with well-established competition.
