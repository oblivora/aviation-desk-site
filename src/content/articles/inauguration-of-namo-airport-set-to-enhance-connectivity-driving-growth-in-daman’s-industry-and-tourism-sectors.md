Daman has never had a scheduled flight of its own — until July 16, 2026. That afternoon, an Alliance Air aircraft lifted off from the newly operational **NAMO Airport** on the inaugural Daman–Delhi–Daman service, giving the Union Territory its first direct air link to the national capital.

## A first for the Union Territory

Union Civil Aviation Minister **Ram Mohan Naidu Kinjarapu** flagged off the flight alongside **Praful Patel**, Administrator of Dadra and Nagar Haveli and Daman and Diu. The route replaces a road journey of **8–10 hours** via Surat or Mumbai with a flight of roughly **2.5 hours**.

> "Delhi will be just 2.5 hours away from Daman," Naidu said at the launch.

## Inside NAMO Airport

NAMO Airport — named in tribute to Prime Minister Narendra Modi — is a dual-use facility built alongside the Indian Coast Guard Air Station (ICGAS) in Daman. Its timeline:

- **April 25, 2023** — Foundation stone for the civil terminal laid by the Prime Minister
- **June 5, 2026** — Airport formally inaugurated by the Prime Minister
- **July 16, 2026** — First scheduled commercial flight takes off, operated by Alliance Air

The terminal spans **3,700 sq. m** on a **25-acre** site, built at a cost of **₹124 crore**, of which the Ministry of Civil Aviation reimbursed **₹88 crore**. It's designed to handle up to **14 ATR aircraft movements a day** and as many as **3.67 lakh passengers annually**.

## Why it matters for industry?

The economic case is built on scale. The minister pointed to more than **7,000 industries** already operating across Daman, Diu and Dadra and Nagar Haveli, with another **15,000-plus** in neighbouring Vapi and Valsad — over **22,000 industrial units** now sitting within reach of same-day air cargo to the rest of India.

Officials expect the fastest gains in sectors that depend on speed to market:

- **Fishery and marine products** — able to reach markets across India within hours instead of days
- **Food processing goods** — faster distribution from a cluster long dependent on road and rail
- **Pharmaceuticals** — a sector the minister said would get fresh momentum from the new air link

Naidu also noted that the Union Territory has drawn infrastructure investment of more than **₹13,000 crore** across hundreds of projects over the past decade, with NAMO Airport positioned as the connective piece tying that investment to the rest of the country.

## Riding on UDAN

The launch lands alongside a bigger policy push: the government has extended the **UDAN regional connectivity scheme by another 10 years**, backed by an outlay of **₹29,000 crore** to build **100 new airports and 200 new helipads** over the next decade. NAMO Airport becomes one of the early additions to that expanded network.

## What's next?

Naidu struck a long-term note at the launch, saying he expects the airport to grow several times over in the next 15 years and become a genuine driver of the region's economy. For now, the Daman–Delhi route is the anchor service — but with Alliance Air already operating and a scheme built to fund dozens more airports like this one, it's unlikely to be the last route out of Daman.
