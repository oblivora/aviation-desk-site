Skyroot Aerospace, a Hyderabad-based space technology company, is scheduled to attempt India's first privately developed orbital rocket launch today, Saturday, July 18, 2026, at 11:30 AM IST (6:00 AM UTC / 2:00 AM EDT). The launch will take place from the First Launch Pad at the Satish Dhawan Space Centre in Sriharikota, Andhra Pradesh, under a mission designated "Aagaman."

## Background

Skyroot was founded in 2018 by Pawan Kumar Chandana and Naga Bharath Daka, both former engineers at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). The company conducted a full-duration static test of its Kalam-100 solid rocket stage on May 19, 2022. On November 18, 2022, Skyroot launched Vikram-S, a suborbital sounding rocket, becoming the first Indian private company to send a vehicle to space. That flight, named "Prarambh," reached an apogee of approximately 89.5 kilometers.

Vikram-1 represents the company's first attempt at an orbital launch. It is designated Test Flight-1 and is the first of three planned development flights intended to validate the vehicle ahead of commercial operations.

## Vehicle Specifications

Vikram-1 is a four-stage launch vehicle approximately 20 meters in height and 1.7 meters in diameter. The vehicle is rated to carry payloads of up to 350 kilograms to low Earth orbit, or 260 kilograms to sun-synchronous orbit. The first three stages — designated Kalam 1200, Kalam 250, and Kalam 100 — use solid propellant. The fourth stage, referred to as the Orbit Adjustment Module, is liquid-fuelled and capable of in-flight restart, allowing the vehicle to deploy payloads into multiple distinct orbits during a single mission.

The vehicle's structure is constructed primarily from carbon-fibre composite material, and its engines are manufactured using additive manufacturing (3D printing). On November 27, 2025, Prime Minister Narendra Modi inaugurated Skyroot's Infinity Campus in Hyderabad and unveiled the flight-ready configuration of Vikram-1. The facility is designed with the capacity to produce one orbital rocket per month.

## Mission Payloads

Mission Aagaman is classified by the company as a technology-demonstration flight, though it carries four customer payloads:

- SCOPE, a technology-demonstration satellite developed by Skyroot
- A hardware demonstration unit provided by DCUBED, a German aerospace manufacturer
- SOLARAS S3, a satellite developed by Grahaa Space, a Bengaluru-based company
- Embrace, an orbital debris-capture robotic arm developed by Cosmoserve Space, based in Hyderabad

The mission also carries non-commercial items, including a handwritten note from Prime Minister Modi and commemorative items referencing Indian scientists Vikram Sarabhai, C.V. Raman, and A.P.J. Abdul Kalam.

## Context and Significance

India's government opened the space sector to private-sector participation on May 16, 2020. Skyroot has since positioned itself as the leading private launch vehicle developer in the country. A successful orbital flight would represent the first instance of an Indian private company independently placing a payload into orbit, without reliance on ISRO-operated launch vehicles.

Company officials have stated that the mission's primary objective is to collect flight performance data on propulsion, staging, guidance, and structural systems under real flight conditions — data that cannot be fully obtained through ground testing. The company has indicated that, contingent on this flight's outcome, it intends to pursue a higher-frequency launch cadence, with a stated goal of producing one orbital rocket per month.

The current launch window opened on July 12, 2026, and remains available through August 4, 2026, providing scheduling flexibility should today's attempt be delayed or postponed.

As of publication, the outcome of the launch attempt had not yet been reported.
